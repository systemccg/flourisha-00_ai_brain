# Deel Health Insurance Options for US Employees: Comprehensive Research Report

**Research Date:** November 26, 2025
**Report Focus:** Health insurance carriers, plans, coverage levels, costs, and comparison to traditional employer plans

---

## Executive Summary

Deel operates as a Professional Employer Organization (PEO) in the United States, providing employers with access to 60+ domestic health insurance plans from major carriers. Deel distinguishes itself through its integrated benefits management platform and exclusive access to Aetna International plans. The platform centralizes enrollment, payroll deductions, and benefits administration while offering employers significant cost savings through group purchasing power compared to traditional self-funded or small-group insurance purchases.

## 2. Coverage Levels and Options

### Core Medical Coverage Options

Deel enables employers to offer the following health insurance plan types:

#### Plan Type Categories

1. **HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)**
   - Coverage limited to designated network providers
   - Lowest premiums among plan types
   - Typically requires primary care physician and referrals
   - Best for: Employees seeking cost control and simplified networks

2. **PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)**
   - Most popular plan type among employees (by wide margin per Deel analysis)
   - Flexibility to see providers inside and outside network
   - Higher premiums than HMO but offer greater provider choice
   - Lower deductibles making them affordable for most workers and families
   - Out-of-network care available at higher out-of-pocket cost

3. **EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization)**
   - Similar to HMO with network-only coverage
   - No referrals required for specialist care
   - Mid-range pricing between HMO and PPO

4. **HDHP (High-Deductible Health Plan)**
   - High deductibles with low monthly premiums
   - Eligible for Health Savings Accounts (HSA)
   - Suitable for: Healthy employees willing to accept higher out-of-pocket costs

### Supplemental Coverage Options

Deel enables employers to offer comprehensive supplementary benefits:

- **Dental Coverage**
  - Preventive care and routine cleanings
  - Restorative procedures
  - Cost range: $10 to several hundred dollars per employee per month
  - Coverage includes exams, procedures, and preventive services

- **Vision Coverage**
  - Eye exams
  - Corrective lenses (glasses and contacts)
  - Cost included in dental/vision packages ($10 to several hundred dollars per employee per month)

- **Life Insurance**
  - Basic and voluntary options available
  - Term life coverage through major carriers

- **Disability Insurance**
  - Short-term disability (STD)
  - Long-term disability (LTD)
  - Coverage through group rates

- **Critical Illness Insurance**
  - Lump-sum benefit upon diagnosis of covered illness
  - Supplements major medical coverage

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts

- **HSA (Health Savings Account)**
  - Triple tax advantage (deductible, grows tax-free, tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses)
  - Only available with HDHP enrollment
  - Employees can use for copays, deductibles, and non-covered services

- **FSA (Flexible Spending Account)**
  - Tax-deductible contributions for medical expenses
  - Annual contribution limits ($3,300 for 2024)
  - Use-it-or-lose-it structure (with carryover options under SECURE 2.0)
  - Covers copays, deductibles, and eligible medical services

- **DCFSA (Dependent Care FSA)**
  - Tax advantages for dependent care expenses
  - Complements medical benefits for working parents

- **Commuter Benefits**
  - Pre-tax transit and parking benefits
  - Reduces taxable income

### Mental Health and Wellness

- ACA-compliant plans include mental health services
- Coverage for therapy, counseling, and psychiatric care
- Employee Assistance Programs (EAP)
- Wellness program benefits

## 3. Costs: Employer and Employee Portions

### Industry Benchmarks (2024)

**Average Annual Costs:**
- Single coverage: ~$9,000 USD
- Family plan coverage: $25,000+ USD

### Cost Structure with Deel PEO

#### Employer Obligations (Legal Requirements)

- **ACA Compliance Requirement:** Employers with 50+ FTE employees must provide affordable coverage
  - Employees cannot pay more than 9.83% of annual salary
  - 95% of employees and their dependents must have access to insurance

#### Cost Sharing Model

**For W-2 Employees:**
- Employers make set contributions to health insurance
- Contributions vary by plan type and carrier selected
- Deel provides transparent side-by-side cost comparison showing:
  - Employee costs for each plan option
  - Employer contribution amounts
  - Coverage details for informed decision-making

**PEO Advantage - Group Rates:**
- PEOs pool workforces across multiple client companies
- Group purchasing power enables negotiation of lower premiums
- Small businesses gain access to large-group rates typically reserved for enterprises
- Cost savings compared to traditional small-group insurance purchases

#### Supplemental Benefits Costs

- **Dental & Vision Packages:** $10 to several hundred dollars per employee per month (varies by plan and coverage level)
- **Life Insurance:** Typically minimal monthly cost ($5-15 per employee)
- **Disability Insurance:** Varies by coverage amount and plan design
- **Critical Illness:** $15-50+ per employee per month depending on benefit amount

### Cost Control Features

1. **Transparent Pricing Display**
   - Deel shows employee and employer costs side-by-side during enrollment
   - Clear visibility into total benefits costs and adoption rates

2. **Local Carrier Carveouts**
   - Support for regionally preferred carriers in specific states
   - Allows honoring employee preferences without disrupting central administration

3. **Administrative Cost Savings**
   - Reduced HR admin burden for benefits management
   - Automatic deduction syncing with payroll
   - Centralized enrollment and renewal processing

## 4. Comparison to Traditional Employer Plans

### Key Differences

#### Traditional Small-Group Plans vs. Deel PEO

| Aspect | Traditional Small Group | Deel PEO |
|--------|------------------------|----------|
| **Carrier Selection** | Limited to available carriers willing to insure small groups | Access to 60+ plans from major carriers |
| **Group Purchasing Power** | Weak negotiating position | Strong - leverages pooled workforce |
| **Premium Costs** | Higher premiums due to poor negotiating leverage | Lower premiums through group rates |
| **Plan Options** | Often 3-5 plan options | Multiple plan types (HMO, PPO, EPO, HDHP) with tiered options |
| **Benefits Administration** | HR department manages | Centralized through Deel platform |
| **Enrollment Process** | Manual, time-consuming | Integrated with payroll and Deel dashboard |
| **Claims Processing** | Multiple touchpoints | Streamlined, reduced friction |
| **International Coverage** | Requires separate provider or manual coordination | Seamless via Aetna International (Deel exclusive) |
| **Tax-Advantaged Accounts** | Available but separate administration | Integrated HSA, FSA, DCFSA management |
| **Renewal Process** | Annual manual coordination with broker | Managed through Deel platform |
| **Global Employees** | Complex, multiple providers needed | Single platform solution with Aetna International |

### PEO Advantages Over Traditional Plans

1. **Economies of Scale**
   - Deel pools employees across dozens of client companies
   - Enables negotiation of enterprise-level rates
   - Small companies (10-100 employees) access Fortune 500-equivalent plans

2. **Administrative Simplification**
   - One integrated platform for payroll, HR, and benefits
   - Automatic payroll deductions synchronized
   - Eliminates need to manage separate benefits brokers and carriers
   - Reduces time spent on benefits administration

3. **Compliance Management**
   - Deel manages ACA compliance (50+ FTE rules)
   - Handles COBRA administration
   - Manages benefits plan design compliance
   - Reduces legal risk for employers

4. **Enhanced Coverage Options**
   - Access to Aetna International (exclusive to Deel)
   - Dental and vision automatically available
   - Wellness and EAP programs included
   - Critical illness and disability options

5. **Cost Visibility**
   - Real-time cost comparison during enrollment
   - Clear employer vs. employee contribution splits
   - Accurate forecasting for budget planning

6. **Coverage Continuity**
   - COBRA administration built-in for terminated employees
   - Continuation coverage for up to 18 months after employment ends
   - Employees can maintain health coverage seamlessly

**Typical Timeline:** 8 weeks for policy opening in the US
