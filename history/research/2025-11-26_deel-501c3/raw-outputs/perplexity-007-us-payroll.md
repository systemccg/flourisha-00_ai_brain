## 1. Payroll Processing Timeline

### 1.1 Regular Payroll Cycle

Deel requires a structured timeline for payroll approval and processing:

**Critical Approval Windows:**
- **Minimum approval window:** 4 days before the employee's pay date
- **Cut-off date:** 5 business days from pay date
- **Payment method:** Direct Debit ACH (default method for US payroll)
- **ACH processing time:** 49 hours to clear before funds are available to Deel
- **Processing funding duration:** Up to 5 business days until accounts are settled

**Employee Payment Delivery:**
- Standard payroll processing: Employees typically receive pay within 5 days of the pay period's end date
- Exact timing depends on the payment method selected

### 1.2 Off-Cycle and Special Payroll Runs

Deel supports flexible payroll scheduling for special circumstances:

**Off-Cycle Processing Timeline:**
- **Processing duration:** Typically 1 business day
- **Approval window:** Approvers have 1 business day to review and approve
- **Payment delivery:** 1-3 business days for payment arrival (when using Deel's Payment by Deel method)

**Use Cases:**
- Bonus payments
- Termination payments
- Emergency advances
- Mid-cycle adjustments
- On-demand payroll options for employees

### 1.3 Key Timeline Factors

The specific timeline is influenced by:
- Payment method selected (ACH vs. other methods)
- Employee bank processing times
- Payroll approval authority availability
- State-specific processing requirements
