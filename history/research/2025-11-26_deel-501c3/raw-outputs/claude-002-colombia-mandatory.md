# Colombia Mandatory Employee Benefits & Deel Management Capabilities
**Research Date:** November 26, 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Colombia has a comprehensive mandatory social security system that employers must provide to all formal employees. The system includes health insurance (EPS), pension funds (AFP), occupational risk insurance (ARL), and additional parafiscal contributions. These benefits represent approximately 40-50% of employee base salary in total employer burden. Deel, as a Global Payroll and Employer of Record (EOR) solution, manages all Colombian mandatory benefits through automated payroll processing and compliance registration.

## Section 1: Colombian Mandatory Employee Benefits

### 1.1 Health Insurance (EPS - Entidades Promotoras de Salud)

**Overview:**
- All formal employees must be affiliated with an EPS provider
- EPS provides access to healthcare services within the mandatory health plan (Plan Obligatorio de Salud - POS)
- Employees have the right to choose their EPS provider

**Contribution Structure:**
- **Total Contribution:** 12.5% of employee monthly salary
  - Employer contribution: 8.5%
  - Employee contribution: 4%

**Mandatory Registration:**
- Employee registration must be completed on the employee's first day of work

---

### 1.2 Pension Funds (AFP - Administradoras de Fondos de Pensiones)

**Overview:**
- Mandatory pension system providing retirement, disability, and survivor benefits
- Multiple private and public AFP providers available
- Employees can select their preferred AFP provider

**Contribution Structure:**
- **Total Contribution:** 16% of monthly salary
  - Employer contribution: 12%
  - Employee contribution: 4%

**Benefits Coverage:**
- Retirement benefits upon reaching pensionable age
- Disability insurance for work-related and non-work-related disabilities
- Survivor benefits for beneficiaries

**Mandatory Registration:**
- Employee registration must be completed on the employee's first day of work

---

### 1.3 Occupational Risk Insurance (ARL - Administradora de Riesgos Laborales)

**Overview:**
- Mandatory insurance protecting employees against work-related injuries, illnesses, and occupational hazards
- Covers medical care, rehabilitation, and disability benefits
- Premium rates vary based on job risk classification

**Contribution Structure:**
- **Employer Coverage:** 100% of contribution (employee does not pay)
- **Contribution Rate Range:** 0.522% to 6.96% of monthly salary
- Rate varies based on:
  - Employee's job title
  - Work activity classification
  - Associated level of occupational risk

**Benefits Coverage:**
- Medical and rehabilitation services for work-related incidents
- Temporary and permanent disability benefits
- Death benefits for families of employees who die from occupational causes

---

### 1.4 Family Compensation Fund (Caja de Compensación Familiar)

**Overview:**
- Parafiscal contribution supporting family welfare services
- Provides family allowances and other social benefits

**Contribution Structure:**
- **Employer Contribution:** 4% of employee monthly salary
- **Employee Contribution:** None
- **Purpose:** Family subsidies and social welfare services

---

### 1.5 National Apprenticeship Service (SENA - Servicio Nacional de Aprendizaje)

**Overview:**
- Parafiscal contribution funding vocational training and apprenticeship programs
- Supports workforce development in Colombia

**Contribution Structure:**
- **Employer Contribution:** 2% of payroll
- **Employee Contribution:** None

**Additional Payments:**
- Annual Law Quote (as of 2023): COP 34,615 per employee

---

### 1.6 Colombian Family Welfare Institute (ICBF - Instituto Colombiano de Bienestar Familiar)

**Overview:**
- Parafiscal contribution supporting child welfare and family services programs

**Contribution Structure:**
- **Employer Contribution:** 3% of employee monthly salary
- **Employee Contribution:** None

---

### 1.7 Vacation Days (Vacaciones)

**Overview:**
- Mandatory paid time off for all formal employees
- Minimum annual entitlement

**Benefits Structure:**
- **Minimum Vacation Days:** 15 business days per year of service
- **Salary Equivalent:** 4.17% of employee's gross annual salary
- **Timing:** Employee can request vacation with employer agreement, typically spread throughout the year

**Accrual & Payment:**
- Employees accrue vacation time throughout the year
- Vacation pay is calculated based on the employee's regular salary
- Unused vacation may carry over to next year (varies by contract)

---

### 1.8 Service Bonus (Prima de Servicios)

**Overview:**
- Mandatory 13th salary bonus, intended as profit-sharing but required regardless of company performance
- One of Colombia's key mandatory benefits

**Benefits Structure:**
- **Amount:** Equivalent to one month's salary (1/12 of annual earnings)
- **Calculation Base:** Includes base salary, bonuses, overtime, and allowances
- **Payment Schedule:**
  - First installment: 50% by June 30th
  - Second installment: 50% by December 20th
- **Timing:** Both installments must be paid on schedule regardless of employment status during the period

**Applicability:**
- Applies to all formal employees with ordinary salaries
- Mandatory payment regardless of company profitability

---

### 1.9 Severance Pay (Cesantía)

**Overview:**
- Mandatory 14th salary compensation providing income security for terminated employees
- Two components: annual accrual and termination indemnity

**Annual Severance Accrual (Cesantía):**
- **Amount:** Equivalent to one month's salary (1/12 of annual earnings)
- **Calculation Base:** Includes base salary, bonuses, overtime, and allowances
- **Accrual Timeline:** Calculated as of December 31st each year
- **Payment Deadline:** Must be deposited into employee's chosen severance fund by February 14th

**Severance Interest (Intereses sobre Cesantía):**
- **Rate:** 12% per year on accrued severance
- **Payment Deadline:** Must be paid directly to employee by January 31st of following year

**Termination Severance Indemnity (When Employment Ends):**
- **Applies When:** Employee is terminated without just cause
- **Does NOT Apply:** When employee resigns voluntarily or is terminated for just cause
- **Payment Structure Varies by Salary:**
  - **For employees earning less than 10x minimum monthly wage:**
    - First year of service: 30 days of salary
    - Each additional year: 20 days of salary
  - **For employees earning more than 10x minimum monthly wage:**
    - First year of service: 20 days of salary
    - Each additional year: 15 days of salary

**Payment Upon Termination:**
- Employees receive accrued cesantía (annual severance)
- Employees receive severance interest owed
- Employees receive vacation pay accrued to termination date
- Employees receive proportional prima (bonus) to termination date
