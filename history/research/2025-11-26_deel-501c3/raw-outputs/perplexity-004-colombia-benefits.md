# Deel Employee Benefits for Colombia-Based Employees and Colombian Labor Law Compliance

**Research Date:** November 26, 2025
**Research Focus:** Deel's benefits offerings for Colombia, compliance with Colombian labor law, and mandatory vs. optional benefits

---

## Executive Summary

Deel provides comprehensive employee benefits management for Colombia-based employees through its Employer of Record (EOR) service. The company ensures full compliance with Colombian labor laws by managing mandatory statutory benefits while offering optional supplementary benefits. This research examines Deel's specific benefits offerings, how they align with Colombian legal requirements, and the employer contribution structure.

## Section 1: Deel's Employee Benefits for Colombian Employees

### 1.1 Statutory (Mandatory) Benefits Provided by Deel

Deel ensures all Colombian employees receive the following mandatory statutory benefits:

#### Paid Time Off
- **Minimum Annual Leave:** 15 working days of paid vacation per year
- **Value:** Equivalent to 4.17% of the employee's gross annual salary
- **Public Holidays:** 18 public holidays annually
- **Parental Leave:** Paid parental leave as required by law

#### Health Insurance
- **Coverage:** Public health insurance through the Sistema General de Seguridad Social en Salud (SGSSS)
- **Employer Contribution:** 8.5% of monthly salary
- **Employee Contribution:** 4% through payroll deduction (handled by Deel)
- **Total Cost:** 12.5% of monthly salary
- **Coverage Scope:** Comprehensive medical services, specialist treatment, and coverage for dependents

#### Pension/Retirement System
- **System:** Sistema General de Pensiones (SGP) with two options:
  - **Public Regime (RPM):** Administered by Colpensiones (defined benefit)
  - **Individual Savings Regime (RAIS):** Administered by private pension funds (defined contribution)
- **Employer Contribution:** 12% of monthly salary
- **Employee Contribution:** 4% through payroll deduction
- **Total Cost:** 16% of monthly salary

#### 13th Month Bonus (Prima de Servicios)
- **Value:** One month's gross salary paid annually
- **Payment Schedule:** Two installments per year
- **Employer Cost:** 1 month salary annually

#### 14th Month Bonus (Severance Fund/Cesantías)
- **Value:** One month's gross salary annually
- **Employer Cost:** 1 month salary annually

#### Interest on Severance Fund
- **Rate:** 12% of the 14th month salary contribution
- **Employer Cost:** Additional monthly expense

#### Occupational Risk Insurance (ARL)
- **Coverage:** Workers' compensation for job-related injuries and illnesses
- **Employer Contribution Rate:** 0.52% to 6.96% based on job classification and risk level
- **Administration:** Handled through mandatory insurance providers

#### Family Compensation Fund (CCF)
- **Employer Contribution:** 4% of monthly salary
- **Purpose:** Provides benefits for education, housing, and family recreation

#### Family Fund (ICBF)
- **Employer Contribution:** 3% of monthly salary
- **Purpose:** Child and family welfare programs

#### Training Fund (SENA)
- **Employer Contribution:** 2% of monthly salary
- **Purpose:** Professional development and job training

#### Work-From-Home Allowance
- **Amount:** USD $32 per month
- **Provided by:** Deel to support remote work infrastructure

## Section 2: Colombian Labor Law Compliance

### 2.1 Legal Framework

Colombian employment law is governed by the **Código Sustantivo del Trabajo (Labor Code)**, which establishes:
- Working hours and breaks
- Minimum wage and compensation rules
- Mandatory benefit requirements
- Termination procedures and severance
- Social security contributions
- Contract requirements

### 2.2 Mandatory Benefits Under Colombian Law

Colombian labor law mandates the following benefits:

#### Social Security System (Sistema General de Seguridad Social - SGSS)
Comprehensive coverage including:
- Healthcare (SGSSS)
- Pensions (SGP)
- Occupational risk coverage (ARL)
- Unemployment insurance (protection fund)

#### Minimum Wage and Compensation
- Annual cost-of-living adjustments
- Payment of 13th and 14th month bonuses
- Overtime compensation at minimum 1.25x hourly rate

#### Leave Entitlements
- 15 days annual paid vacation
- 18 public holidays
- Parental leave (maternal and paternal)
- Sick leave with medical certification

#### Working Hours
- Maximum 48 hours per week
- Mandatory breaks and rest periods
- Additional compensation for overtime

### 2.3 Recent Labor Law Changes (2025)

**June 25, 2025 Labor Reforms** introduced significant changes:
- Enhanced employee protections
- Limits on fixed-term contracts
- New legal standards for teleworking arrangements
- Stricter enforcement of gig economy and dependent contractor protections
- Law 221 of 2021 strengthening worker protections is being enforced more strictly

### 2.4 Compliance Risks

**Key Compliance Issues:**
- Employee misclassification (treating employees as contractors)
- Incorrect statutory benefit calculations
- Non-compliance with contribution requirements
- Failure to maintain proper employment contracts
- Inadequate record-keeping for social security contributions
- Non-compliance with teleworking standards

## Section 3: Mandatory vs. Optional Benefits Framework

### 3.1 Mandatory Benefits (Non-negotiable)

These benefits are required by Colombian law and must be provided to all employees:

1. **Health Insurance (SGSSS)** - 12.5% total contribution
2. **Pension System (SGP)** - 16% total contribution
3. **Occupational Risk Insurance (ARL)** - 0.52%-6.96% based on risk
4. **Paid Annual Leave** - Minimum 15 days
5. **13th Month Bonus** - One month salary
6. **14th Month Bonus (Severance Fund)** - One month salary
7. **Interest on Severance** - 12% of severance contribution
8. **Family Compensation Fund (CCF)** - 4% contribution
9. **Family Fund (ICBF)** - 3% contribution
10. **Training Fund (SENA)** - 2% contribution
11. **Public Holiday Compensation** - 18 days annually
12. **Parental Leave** - As defined by law

### 3.2 Optional Benefits (Employer discretion)

These benefits can be added by employers to enhance their compensation packages:

1. Private health insurance supplements
2. Life insurance
3. Dental insurance
4. Disability/accident insurance
5. Performance bonuses
6. Extra paid time off
7. Meal vouchers
8. Transportation allowances
9. Gym memberships
10. Childcare assistance
11. Stock options
12. Professional development stipends
13. Flexible work arrangements
14. Coworking space access

### 3.3 Key Distinction

**Mandatory benefits** are calculated as percentages of salary or fixed amounts and must be paid to/for every employee regardless of tenure or position. **Optional benefits** are discretionary add-ons that employers can use to enhance compensation and competitiveness while maintaining compliance with minimum legal requirements.

## Section 4: How Deel Manages Colombian Benefits Compliance

### 4.1 Employer of Record (EOR) Model

Deel operates as the legal employer of record for employees in Colombia while the hiring company maintains operational control. This model provides:

- **Legal Protection:** Deel assumes employment-related legal liability
- **Compliance Management:** Deel ensures all statutory requirements are met
- **Contract Administration:** Deel creates and manages employment contracts compliant with Colombian law
- **Payroll Processing:** Automated, compliant payroll calculations
- **Contribution Management:** All social security, pension, and tax contributions are handled
- **Benefit Administration:** All mandatory and optional benefits are tracked and delivered

### 4.2 Compliance Features

#### Regulatory Monitoring
- Continuous tracking of Colombian labor law changes
- Automatic updates to contract templates when regulations change
- Real-time compliance monitoring to ensure no violations

#### Local Expertise
- Deel maintains a wholly-owned Colombian legal entity
- Staffed with local legal experts familiar with Colombian employment law
- Dedicated onboarding managers who ensure local law compliance

#### Automated Processes
- Contract generation adapted to local labor laws
- Automated payroll calculations including all mandatory contributions
- Benefit deduction and payment automation
- Tax and contribution filing

#### Record Keeping and Documentation
- Maintenance of compliant employment records
- Documentation of all contributions and benefit payments
- Preparation for government audits and inspections

### 4.3 Employee Data Protection Compliance

Deel ensures compliance with Colombian data protection laws while managing employee information necessary for benefits administration, payroll, and tax purposes.

### 4.4 Law 221 Compliance (Gig Economy Protection)

With increased enforcement of Law 221 of 2021 regarding dependent contractor protections, Deel helps ensure that employee relationships are properly classified as employment rather than independent contractor arrangements, preventing misclassification penalties.

### 4.5 Teleworking Compliance

Following the June 2025 labor reforms introducing new legal standards for teleworking, Deel's platform ensures:
- Proper telework agreement documentation
- Compliance with new teleworking standards
- Appropriate equipment provisions (including work-from-home allowances)
