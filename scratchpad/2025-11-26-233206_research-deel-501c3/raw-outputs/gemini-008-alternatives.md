- **Best for Small Nonprofits (4 employees):** Gusto

- **Best for Volume & Growth:** Papaya Global

## Detailed Platform Analysis

### 1. GUSTO - Best for Small Nonprofits

**Target Market:** Small to medium-sized nonprofits, particularly US-focused

#### Pricing for Nonprofits
- **Base:** $40-49/month
- **Per Employee:** $6/month additional
- **4-Employee Nonprofit Cost:** ~$64-73/month
- **Special Nonprofit Offering:** Base fee waived for first 10 employees
- **International Contractors:** $120+ countries, no monthly per-contractor fee (only service + FX fees)

#### Key Features
- **Payroll & Compliance:** Automatic federal, state, local tax filing
- **Tax Documents:** W-2s, 1099s, new hire reports automatically generated
- **Unlimited Payroll Runs:** No additional charges for off-cycle payrolls
- **Built-in Time Tracking:** Integrated hours logging for grant reporting
- **Benefits Administration:** Health insurance brokering at no extra cost (pay only premiums)
- **Global Contractors:** Supports 120+ countries for contractor payments

#### International Employee Management
- **Limited Direct Support:** International employees handled through Gusto Global (powered by Remote)
- **Gusto Global Pricing:** $599/month per international employee
- **Hybrid Model:** Domestic payroll through Gusto, international through Remote integration

#### Nonprofit-Specific Strengths
- Purpose-built nonprofit solutions with compliance focused on grant reporting
- Affordable pricing tier for organizations with limited budgets
- Time tracking directly supports grant administration requirements
- Ranked #1 on G2 Fall 2025 for satisfaction

#### Pros & Cons
**Pros:**
- Extremely affordable for small nonprofits
- Intuitive interface, minimal setup friction
- No surprise fees or hidden costs
- Excellent for US-based payroll
- Built-in features specifically for nonprofit workflows

**Cons:**
- Limited native international payroll capabilities
- International employees require separate Gusto Global service ($599/month minimum)
- Customer service historically slow (11+ hours reported for some setups)
- Becomes cost-prohibitive if scaling internationally beyond contractors

#### Recommendation: IDEAL for 4-person nonprofit if primarily US-based with occasional international contractors

### 2. RIPPLING - Best for Integrated HR/IT Management

**Target Market:** Growing companies needing comprehensive workforce management

#### Pricing Structure
- **Base:** $35-40/month (varies by module)
- **Per Employee:** $8/month
- **4-Employee Nonprofit Cost:** ~$67-72/month (base + 4 × $8)
- **International EOR:** Custom pricing (example: 50 employees + 10 international = ~$7,390/month)
- **Nonprofit Discounts:** Contact sales (varies)

#### Key Features
- **All-in-One Platform:** Integrates HR, payroll, IT device management, and benefits
- **Global Payroll:** 185+ countries, 50+ currencies in single workflow
- **Compliance:** 90+ countries with automatic tax calculations
- **Device Management:** IT management alongside HR (unique feature)
- **Employee Self-Service:** Portal for employees to manage documents, time-off
- **Benefits Administration:** Health insurance, retirement, voluntary benefits

#### International Capabilities
- **Countries Covered:** 185+ countries for payroll operations
- **Currencies:** 50+ currencies supported
- **EOR Services:** Available but requires custom quote
- **Compliance Engine:** Automatic tax calculations and compliance checks

#### Nonprofit-Specific Considerations
- Not specifically designed for nonprofits
- Discount availability unclear (requires sales conversation)
- Overkill for small teams but scales excellently

#### Pros & Cons
**Pros:**
- Most comprehensive platform combining HR, IT, and payroll
- Scales beautifully from 4 to 400+ employees
- Modular pricing (pay only for what you use)
- Strong international capabilities
- Excellent for tech-forward nonprofits

**Cons:**
- Longer onboarding (3-8 weeks typical due to integrations)
- Pricing complexity makes budgeting harder
- More enterprise-focused, potentially overkill for 4-person team
- Nonprofit discount details not publicly available

#### Recommendation: GOOD for nonprofits planning significant growth or needing IT management integration

## Recommendations by Use Case

### For a 4-Person Nonprofit, US-Based Only
**BEST CHOICE: Gusto**
- Cost: ~$65-80/month
- Reason: Unbeatable pricing, nonprofit-specific features, easy setup
- With international contractors: Add ~$0-30/month (FX fees only)

### For a 4-Person Nonprofit with 1-2 International Employees
**BEST CHOICE: Oyster**
- Cost: ~$1,300-1,400/month (4 × $25 payroll + 2 × $599 EOR)
- Reason: Strongest nonprofit support, transparent pricing, mission alignment
- Alternative: Remote.com for equivalent features with more transparency

### For Rapid Growth (Scaling from 4 to 20+ Employees)
**BEST CHOICE: Rippling**
- Cost: Scales predictably with per-employee model
- Reason: Best integration, IT management included, grows with you
- Note: Longer onboarding required

### For Mission-Driven International Hiring
**BEST CHOICE: Remote for Good** (Remote.com's nonprofit program)
- Cost: 15% discount on EOR services + free HR tools
- Reason: Explicitly designed for nonprofits, community support, values alignment

### For Enterprise-Scale Nonprofit (50+ Employees)
**BEST CHOICE: Papaya Global**
- Cost: Tiered volume pricing, most country coverage
- Reason: Best for complex global operations, AI compliance, expert support

## Implementation Notes for Nonprofits

### Tax Compliance Advantages by Platform
- **Gusto:** Best 990-N, 990-EZ, 990 reporting support
- **Rippling:** Good general compliance, enterprise-grade
- **Remote/Oyster:** Good international compliance, weaker US 990 integration
- **Papaya:** Best for complex multi-country compliance

### Data Security & Nonprofit Considerations
- All five platforms maintain SOC 2 compliance
- Remote and Oyster explicitly support nonprofit data privacy concerns
- Rippling most comprehensive with IT management (device security)

### Support Quality Notes
- **Excellent:** Rippling (enterprise support), Oyster (nonprofit-focused)
- **Good:** Remote (responsive), Papaya (enterprise support)
- **Potential Issues:** Gusto (reported slow support despite good platform)

---

## Conclusion

**For a small nonprofit with 4 US-based employees:** Gusto provides the best combination of affordability, ease of use, and nonprofit-specific features at ~$65-80/month.

**For a nonprofit managing international teams:** Remote.com (with Remote for Good discount) or Oyster offer the best balance of features, affordability, and nonprofit support, though costs increase to $600+/month for each international employee.

**For nonprofits planning significant growth or needing IT management:** Rippling offers the most comprehensive platform, though at higher cost and longer implementation time.

**For large-scale international nonprofits:** Papaya Global provides the broadest country coverage and most sophisticated compliance automation.
