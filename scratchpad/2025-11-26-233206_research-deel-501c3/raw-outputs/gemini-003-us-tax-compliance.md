## 1. Federal Payroll Tax Handling

### 1.1 FICA Tax Compliance

Deel automatically calculates and withholds FICA taxes (Social Security and Medicare) according to 2025 IRS requirements:

- **Social Security Tax:** 6.2% employee withholding on wages up to the 2025 wage base limit of $176,100
- **Medicare Tax:** 1.45% employee withholding on all wages with no income cap
- **Additional Medicare Tax:** 0.9% withheld from employees earning over $200,000 annually
- **Employer Matching:** Deel calculates employer's matching contribution (6.2% Social Security + 1.45% Medicare)
- **Total FICA Rate:** 15.3% (split evenly between employer and employee)

**Capability:** Deel's platform instantly calculates payroll taxes and handles all FICA withholding requirements with full IRS compliance.

### 1.2 Federal Income Tax Withholding

- Automatic calculation based on current federal withholding tax tables (updated for 2025)
- W-4 form integration and management
- Real-time adjustment capabilities for changing withholding preferences
- Support for multiple filing statuses and allowances

### 1.3 Federal Unemployment Tax (FUTA)

- Deel handles Form 940 (Annual Unemployment Tax Return) filing
- Automatic FUTA calculation at required federal rate
- Annual filing deadline management (January 31 or February 10 if taxes deposited timely)

### 1.4 Tax Deposit Scheduling

- Automated deposit scheduling based on employer's tax liability classification
- Semi-weekly and monthly deposit requirements handled automatically
- Integration with IRS payment systems
- Real-time tax liability tracking

## 3. Multi-State Payroll Capabilities

### 3.1 Remote Employee Tax Handling

**Withholding Rule:** Tax should be withheld for the state where services are performed, not necessarily where the employee resides or the employer is located.

**Deel's Approach:**
- Tracks employee work location in payroll system
- Calculates state tax withholding based on actual work location
- Updates withholding when employees work in multiple states
- Handles reciprocal state agreements automatically

### 3.2 Multiple State Work Scenarios

Deel supports complex employment scenarios:
- Employees working in multiple states during a single pay period
- Employees with changing work locations (office, remote, rotating locations)
- Partial-year work in different states
- Temporary assignments across state lines

### 3.3 Tax Withholding Complexity Management

**Challenges Deel Addresses:**
1. **Nexus Establishment:** Automatically establishes tax nexus when employee performs work in a state
2. **Multi-Location Tracking:** Maintains records of employee work time by state
3. **Reciprocal Agreement Processing:** Applies interstate tax reciprocity agreements where applicable
4. **Wage Allocation:** Allocates wages to correct states for tax calculation

### 3.4 Common Pitfall Prevention

Deel prevents common multi-state payroll errors:
- Misconfigured home addresses vs. actual work locations
- Incorrect State Unemployment Insurance (SUI) tax calculations
- Wrong state income tax withholding rates
- Late multi-state registration penalties
- Unexpected multi-state tax liabilities

## 4. Tax Filing and Reporting

### 4.1 Quarterly Federal Tax Filing (Form 941)

**Coverage:** Deel handles complete Form 941 Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return processing

**What Form 941 Reports:**
- Employee wages, tips, and other compensation
- Federal income tax withheld from employee wages
- Social Security and Medicare taxes (FICA)

**Filing Deadlines (Quarterly):**
- Q1: April 30
- Q2: July 31
- Q3: October 31
- Q4: January 31 (following year)

**Deel's Process:**
- Automatic calculation of all required Form 941 data
- Real-time reconciliation with payroll records
- Automatic filing with IRS
- Employer and employee record maintenance

### 4.2 Annual Unemployment Tax Filing (Form 940)

**Coverage:** Deel handles annual FUTA tax reporting

**Filing Deadline:** January 31 (or February 10 if taxes were deposited timely)

**Deel's Capabilities:**
- Automatic FUTA calculation for the full year
- Form 940 completion and filing
- Integration with quarterly Form 941 totals for reconciliation

### 4.3 W-2 (Employee Wages and Tax Statement)

**Deel's W-2 Capabilities:**

- Employee access to W-2 forms for personal record-keeping

**Deadline:** W-2s must be distributed by January 31 of the following tax year

### 4.5 Tax Reporting and Payroll Reconciliation

## 10. Recommendations for 501(c)(3) Organizations

Based on this research, here are considerations for nonprofits using Deel:

### 10.1 Tax Filing Considerations
- Nonprofits must still withhold and file all federal and state payroll taxes (FICA, FUTA, SUI)
- Tax-exempt status does not eliminate payroll tax obligations
- Deel's automation ensures nonprofit compliance with all payroll tax requirements

### 10.2 Form 990 Integration
- Ensure payroll records from Deel integrate with Form 990 reporting requirements
- Maintain detailed records of compensation for highly paid employees
- Document Form 941 filings for consistency with annual Form 990 filing

### 10.3 Multi-State Operations
- If the nonprofit operates in multiple states or has remote employees across states, Deel's multi-state capabilities are valuable
- Ensure proper state registration and SUI contributions in each operating state
- Maintain records of employee work locations for tax withholding accuracy

### 10.4 Contractor vs. Employee Classification
- Nonprofits frequently misclassify workers as contractors
- Deel's guidance on proper classification helps avoid IRS penalties
- Document the basis for contractor vs. employee classification
