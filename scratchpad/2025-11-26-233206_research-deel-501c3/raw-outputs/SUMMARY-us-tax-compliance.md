## Multi-State Payroll

### Key Rule
**Tax is withheld for the STATE WHERE SERVICES ARE PERFORMED** (not employee residence or employer location)

### Deel's Multi-State Capabilities
✓ Tracks employee work location in payroll
✓ Calculates state tax based on actual work location
✓ Handles employees working in multiple states in single pay period
✓ Manages reciprocal state tax agreements automatically
✓ Allocates wages correctly to each state for tax purposes

### Common Pitfall Prevention
- Prevents mismatched home address vs. work location errors
- Ensures correct SUI tax calculation
- Avoids incorrect state income tax withholding
- Prevents late multi-state registration penalties

## Tax Filing & Reporting

### Quarterly Filing
**Form 941** - Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return
- Due: April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31
- Reports: Federal income tax withheld, Social Security & Medicare taxes
- **Deel Handling:** Automatic calculation, filing, and reconciliation

### Annual Filing
**Form 940** - Annual Unemployment Tax Return
- Due: January 31 (or Feb 10 if taxes deposited timely)
- Reports: Federal unemployment (FUTA) tax
- **Deel Handling:** Automatic FUTA calculation and Form 940 filing

### Employee Tax Documents
**Form W-2** - Wages and Tax Statement
- Due: January 31 (to employees and Social Security Administration)
- **Deel Handling:** Auto-generated, multi-state calculations, direct SSA filing

**Form 1099-NEC** - Non-Employee Compensation (contractors paid $600+)
- Due: January 31 (January 1-31 filing window)
- **Deel Handling:** Auto-generated, filed with IRS, copies to contractor, email notifications

### Accounting Integration
- Real-time payroll data syncs to accounting software
- Direct deposit and payslip synchronization
- Compliance-ready records for audits
- Historical data retention

## Critical Compliance Checklist for Payroll Managers

Using Deel for US Payroll Tax Compliance:

- [ ] Ensure accurate employee classification (W-2 vs. 1099)
- [ ] Verify employee work location configured correctly (not just home address)
- [ ] Confirm W-4 information is current for each employee
- [ ] Monitor for regulatory changes flagged by Deel's compliance alerts
- [ ] Maintain backup records of payroll history for audit purposes
- [ ] Track Form 941 quarterly filings against annual W-2 totals
- [ ] Ensure state registrations are current for all operating locations
- [ ] Review year-end reconciliation (Form 941 total = W-2 total for each EIN)
- [ ] Verify direct deposit and payslip syncs to accounting software
- [ ] Keep contractor payment records for $600+ threshold tracking
