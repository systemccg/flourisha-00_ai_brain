## Market Analysis & Insights

### Why King Size Murphy Beds Are Rare

1. **Engineering Challenge:** King mattresses (76"W √ó 80"L) require reinforced mechanisms
2. **Space Requirements:** Need 90"+ wall space and 10+ foot ceilings
3. **Weight Capacity:** Require 2,000-2,500 lbs capacity (higher cost mechanisms)
4. **Material Costs:** 20-30% more materials than queen size
5. **Limited Demand:** Smaller market segment (most buyers choose queen)

### Pricing Factor Breakdown

| Factor | Impact on Price | Example |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Materials** | 40% | MDF ($1,500) vs Solid Hardwood ($4,000+) |
| **Mechanism** | 25% | Manual spring (baseline) vs Gas piston (+$800) vs Electric (+$2,000) |
| **Size Premium** | 20% | King size adds 20-40% to queen pricing |
| **Customization** | 10% | Built-in desk (+$400-800), storage (+$300-1,200) |
| **Installation** | 5% | DIY ($0) vs Professional ($300-$1,500) |

### Market Positioning

**Direct-to-Consumer Dominance:** Wilding, BredaBeds, Lori Beds, Murphy Bed Depot all use factory-direct models to reduce costs 20-30% vs traditional retail.

**King Size Gap:** Only 3-4 major manufacturers offer true pre-built king size Murphy beds, creating a niche market with less competition.

**Customization Trend:** BredaBeds' 30,000+ configuration strategy is gaining traction as buyers demand personalization.
